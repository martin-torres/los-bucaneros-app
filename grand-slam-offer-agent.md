## 2. PRICING RECOMMENDATION

### Setup Fee: **$0. Never charge it.**

Fixed costs create friction. A $5,000 setup fee loses you 50% of prospects before they see any value. Zero setup removes the single largest objection for cash-tight restaurant owners who've been burned by tech salesmen.

If they leave after 3 months, you lose your effort. With the guarantee (see Section 5), the ones who stay are the ones who get value. This is a feature, not a bug.

### Transaction Fee: **5% introductory -> 8% standard**

| Why 5% for early adopters | Why not lower | Why not higher |
|---|---|---|
| Removes price objection for first customers | Free signals no confidence in product | 8% is still half of what delivery apps charge |
| Creates urgency to join "before price goes up" | 3% doesn't leave room for future raises | Above 10% approaches waiter tip territory (wrong comparison) |
| Grandfathering at 5% builds loyalty and referrals | 5% still generates meaningful revenue at volume | Above 10% makes delivery app comparison less dramatic |

### The Framing Analysis: **Do NOT compare to waiter tips. Compare to delivery app commissions.**

**Wrong framing: "It replaces the waiter's tip"**
- The customer pays the tip, not the restaurant owner. The owner doesn't feel that cost as an expense. It never hits their bank account.
- Waiters are real people your customer sees every shift. Framing your product as "waiter replacement" signals to staff that you're trying to eliminate jobs. Creates hostility at the point of use.
- Tips are psychologically "gratitude money," not operating expense. Comparing your SaaS fee to a tip makes you look greedy, not economical.

**Right framing: "It replaces the 25-30% you're giving to delivery apps"**
- The owner WRITES THAT CHECK every month. It hurts. They can tell you the exact percentage without looking it up.
- Direct comparison: 25-30% vs 5-8%. The math is undeniable in 2 seconds.
- Delivery apps are deeply hated. You're not replacing a person — you're killing a parasitic middleman.
- Same type of transaction (digital, customer-facing, automated). Clean apples-to-apples comparison.

**Secondary frame: "It replaces the $8,000/mo salary you're paying someone to type WhatsApp orders"**
- This is direct payroll savings. The owner feels this every pay period.
- Combined killer frame: "You're bleeding 25-30% to Uber Eats AND paying someone full-time to copy-paste orders from your phone. We replace both for 5-8%."

## 3. FIRST-CUSTOMER STRATEGY

### The Offer for Customers 1-10

| Element | Terms | Why |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Setup fee | **$0** (waived) | Remove the #1 objection. They've been burned by tech salesmen before. Zero cost to try. |
| Transaction fee | **5%** (grandfathered forever) | Below target price. Makes them feel like insiders, not guinea pigs. |
| Guarantee | **60-day full refund, keep the hardware** | Complete risk elimination for a product with no track record. |
| Their commitment | **One 30-minute filmed testimonial at day 60** (if happy) | You get your first case studies. This is how you sell to customers 11-100. |

### Handling "No Track Record"

**Don't defend it. Reverse it.**

"Of course we have no track record. That's exactly why you get 5% forever, zero setup cost, and a full money-back guarantee. We're betting on YOU. If we work, you win. If we don't, you lose nothing. We're the ones taking all the risk."

This frames the lack of track record as YOUR problem (their objection is valid), and you're "solving" it by over-delivering on terms. The asymmetry of risk works in your favor.

### Grandfather Clause: **YES — and it's your #1 closing tool.**

"Join now and your 5% rate is locked in forever. Even when we raise prices to 8%, 10%, or higher for new customers — your rate never changes. This is your reward for believing first."

This creates:
- **Urgency** — don't miss the window
- **Loyalty** — they feel like founders, not customers
- **Word-of-mouth** — they will brag to other owners about getting the early rate

### What NOT to Do

**Do NOT do a "free trial" where they pay nothing for 30 days.** Without payment, there's zero commitment. They won't train staff, won't push customers to use it, won't implement properly. Skin in the game is necessary for adoption. The 60-day guarantee IS your trial — but with payment flowing from day 1 so they're incentived to make it work.

- Inventory tracking (what sold, what's left, auto-reorder suggestions)
- Staff scheduling and shift management overlay

**Pricing for new customers:** 8% stays standard for single-location. Grandfathered 5% customers keep their rate. Phase 1 customers who joined at 8% also keep 8%.

### Phase 3: Premium Tier (Months 12+)

Once the platform has depth:

| Tier | Rate | Best for |
|------|:----:|----------|
| **Standard** | 8% | Single-location, basic ordering + database |
| **Premium** | $5,000/mo flat + 3% | Chains (3+ locations). Includes marketing automation, multi-location analytics, dedicated account manager, priority support |

**Hard grandfather rule:** Phase 1 customers stay at 5% forever. Phase 2 customers stay at 8% forever. Only new customers see Phase 3 pricing.

### How to Raise Without Burning Early Adopters

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Never raise rates on existing customers.** Ever. | Your early customers are your marketing department. They will refer 10x their lifetime value. Treat them like royalty. |
| **Raise by adding, not by increasing.** | New features = new tier. Don't touch the base rate. Create premium add-ons for power users. |
| **Communicate pride, not apology.** | "We've added so much value that we're introducing a premium tier for growing restaurants. Your current plan stays exactly as it is. If you ever want more, here's what's available." |
| **Scarcity to close, not to pressure.** | "Founders rate closes in 30 days" is ethical closing. "Sign now or you'll regret it" is pressure. Use the former. |

## 5. RISK REVERSAL

### The Guarantee (Single Sentence. No Fine Print.)

> "If after 60 days you haven't saved MORE in reduced delivery app commissions and labor than our total fee, we'll refund every centavo, remove the system, and you keep the QR codes as our gift for trying. You literally cannot lose."

### Why This Works

| Element | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **60 days** | Long enough for real results. Short enough for cash-flow comfort. Two full pay cycles. |
| **"Save MORE than our fee"** | Ties refund to hard ROI, not subjective satisfaction. If they got value, you keep the money. If not, they're whole. Zero disputes possible. |
| **"Keep the QR codes"** | Even in the "loser" scenario, they walk away with free printed QR codes. Removes the fear of "what if I have to undo everything." |
| **"You literally cannot lose"** | The closing anchor. The sentence that kills the final objection. |

### What NOT to Guarantee

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| "X% increase in revenue" | You can't control their pricing, marketing, or execution. Sets you up for disputes. |
| "X hours saved per week" | Hard to measure objectively. "Well I still spend time on X" arguments. |
| "Money back if not satisfied" | Too vague. Satisfaction is subjective. "Save more than our fee" is objectively measurable. |

### How to Handle a Refund Request

Don't fight it. Don't negotiate. Process it immediately.

"Here's your full refund. No questions asked. System will be removed within 24 hours. You keep the QR codes. If you ever want to come back, the founders rate is gone — but we'd love to have you at 8%."

This builds so much trust that a meaningful percentage of refund-takers come back within 60 days on their own. The ones who don't weren't your ideal customers.

## 7. OPEN QUESTIONS

These need founder input before the offer is locked:

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| **What is the actual marginal cost per transaction?** (payment processing + hosting + support per order) | Sets the pricing floor. If processing costs 3.5%, you need at least 5% to survive. If it's 0.5%, you have room. |
| **Is payment processing included in the platform, or does the restaurant use their own POS/terminal?** | If you're not processing payments, the "8% of revenue" model is pure SaaS margin with no pass-through — changes the unit economics. |
| **What hardware does Los Bucaneros provide vs what the restaurant buys?** (kitchen screen? thermal printer? tablets?) | If the founder is providing hardware, the zero-setup model has real up-front cost. If the restaurant uses their own phone/tablet, it's pure software — much easier to risk-reverse. |
| **Is the Telegram integration production-ready?** | If it's still being built, "we start tomorrow" is a promise you can't keep. Verify before making timeline claims. |
| **CFDI compliance — has this been verified with a Mexican accountant or SAT directly?** | If the digital receipts have legal gaps, this is a liability, not a feature. Get external validation before promising it. |
| **What is the average monthly transaction volume for the first 3 target restaurants?** | Confirms whether the 2.6x value ratio holds in reality, or needs recalibration for smaller restaurants with lower volume. |
| **Is the entire owner experience in Spanish, or is there any English in the dashboard?** | If there's any English in the dashboard or setup flow, it's a dealbreaker for this market. Verify before promising "simple." |
